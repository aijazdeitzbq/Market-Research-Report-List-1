<p><ul><li>Cast Aluminum</li><li>Rolled Aluminum</li><li>Extruded Aluminum</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The aluminum alloy automotive sheet market consists of three primary types: cast aluminum, rolled aluminum, and extruded aluminum. Cast aluminum involves pouring molten metal into molds, resulting in complex shapes suitable for various automotive components. Rolled aluminum is produced by passing aluminum through rollers, yielding thin sheets ideal for body panels and structural applications. Extruded aluminum is formed by forcing metal through a die, creating long shapes used in frames and supports. Each type offers unique benefits in strength, weight reduction, and design flexibility.</p></p>
