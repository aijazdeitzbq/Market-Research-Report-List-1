TRIF utilizes fluorescent labels and specific antibodies to measure enzyme levels with high sensitivity and accuracy, making it suitable for detecting specific isoenzymes. CLIA, on the other hand, employs luminous reactions to quantify enzyme levels, offering rapid results and a wide detection range. Both methods are essential for diagnosing cardiac and muscle disorders based on creatine kinase isoenzyme profiles.</p></p>
