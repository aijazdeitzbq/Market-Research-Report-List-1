<p><strong>The Dental Casting Porcelain Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>High Fusing</li><li>Medium Fusing</li><li>Low Fusing</li><li>Ultra-Low Fusing</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dental casting porcelain market comprises four main types based on their fusing temperatures. High fusing porcelain, typically used for metal-ceramic restorations, withstands temperatures above 1300°C. Medium fusing porcelain, ideal for various applications, operates between 1100°C and 1300°C. Low fusing porcelain, which melts between 850°C and 1100°C, is often used for denture teeth. Ultra-low fusing porcelain, below 850°C, is utilized for specific aesthetic applications, allowing for better layering and a more lifelike appearance in dental restorations.</p></p>
