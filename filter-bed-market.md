<p><p>Filter beds are categorized into two primary types: natural and artificial. Natural filter beds utilize naturally occurring materials such as sand, gravel, and organic matter to purify water by allowing it to pass through these layers, trapping impurities and supporting microbial activity. In contrast, artificial filter beds are engineered systems designed with specific materials and configurations to optimize filtration efficiency. Both types aim to enhance water quality by removing contaminants, though the approach and materials differ significantly.</p></p>
