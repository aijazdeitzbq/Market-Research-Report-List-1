<p><p>The GPS tracker market is segmented into three main types: standalone trackers, OBD (On-Board Diagnostics) trackers, and advanced trackers. Standalone trackers are portable devices that can be used for personal or asset tracking. OBD trackers plug into a vehicle's OBD-II port, providing real-time location data along with vehicle diagnostics. Advanced trackers offer sophisticated features like geo-fencing, driver behavior analysis, and integration with smart systems, catering to both personal and commercial needs for enhanced tracking and monitoring capabilities.</p></p>
