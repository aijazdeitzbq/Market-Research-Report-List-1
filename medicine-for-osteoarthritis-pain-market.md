<p><p>The osteoarthritis pain management market is categorized into three main types: oral, injection, and external treatments. Oral medications include nonsteroidal anti-inflammatory drugs (NSAIDs) and analgesics taken by mouth for systemic relief. Injection therapies involve corticosteroids or hyaluronic acid delivered directly into the joints for localized pain reduction. External treatments encompass topical analgesics and transdermal patches applied to the skin, providing targeted relief with fewer systemic side effects. Each type offers distinct advantages depending on patient needs and severity of the condition.</p></p>
