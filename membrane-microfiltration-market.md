<p><p>The membrane microfiltration market can be segmented into organic and inorganic types. Organic membranes, typically made from polymers, are flexible and cost-effective, widely used in water treatment and food processing due to their ability to filter out bacteria and suspended solids. In contrast, inorganic membranes, crafted from materials like ceramics or metals, offer superior thermal stability and chemical resistance, making them suitable for harsh environments and industrial applications. Both types play crucial roles in enhancing filtration efficiency across various industries.</p></p>
