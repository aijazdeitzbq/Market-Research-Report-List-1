<p><p>The sweeteners market for food and beverages encompasses various types, including oligosaccharides, inulin, sugar alcohols, and others. Oligosaccharides are short carbohydrate chains that provide sweetness and functional benefits, while inulin, a soluble fiber, contributes to texture and sweetness without raising blood sugar levels. Sugar alcohols, such as erythritol and xylitol, offer sweetness with fewer calories and reduced glycemic impact. Other sweeteners may include natural extracts and artificial sweeteners, each catering to different dietary needs and consumer preferences.</p></p>
