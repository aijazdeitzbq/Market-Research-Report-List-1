<p><p>Ultrasound Non-destructive Testing (NDT) is a method used to evaluate materials and structures without causing damage. It includes various techniques: </p><p>- Ultrasonic Testing employs high-frequency sound waves to detect internal flaws.</p><p>- Radiography Testing uses X-rays or gamma rays for imaging.</p><p>- Visual Inspection Testing involves direct observation of surfaces.</p><p>- Magnetic Particle Testing detects surface and near-surface defects in ferromagnetic materials.</p><p>- Electromagnetic Testing assesses conductivity and material properties.</p><p>- Eddy-Current Testing identifies flaws by inducing currents in conductive materials.</p><p>- Liquid Penetrant Testing reveals surface-breaking defects using dye solutions.</p><p>- Others encompass additional methods like acoustic emission and thermography.</p></p>
